Workshop Information

This workshop covers the basics of Authentication and Authorisation Infrastructure (AAI), getting started with Shibboleth and setting up Identity Providers (IdPs) and Service Providers (SPs).

The workshop is intended for organizations that provide resources (e.g. research data) and services (e.g. processing of the research data) for scientific research and education in various fields, such as bioinformatics, high energy physics, arts and humanities and so forth.

The aim of this training is to help the resource and service providers to make their resources and services available to the users through identity federations. If the concept of the identity federation is not familiar to the reader, the reader should first read the training module Federations 101 that explains the basic concepts of federated identity.

The workshop is a mix of lectures and hands-on lessons where participants will have an apportunity to play around with installing and setting up code.

Schedule

The workshop has two parts: Day 1 is common to all participants. Day 2 will have parallel sessions to cover more community specific topics.

Day 1: Providing online services to a SAML based identity federation using Shibboleth software

A resource or service provider needs to install a server called Service Provider (SP) and integrate it with his or her service in order to securely provide resources and services to a SAML based identity federation online. In this submodule the participants install, test and configure a Service Provider using open source Shibboleth SP software. The goal is to make the participants familiar with the basics configuration of Shibboleth SP. At the end of the training participants should be familiar with the basic aspects of running a Shibboleth SP.

Main Goals of the Training

  • Install and configure a Shibboleth Service Provider 2
  • Know how and where to configure basic functionalities
  • Learn how to protect static web pages and applications
  • Understand how attributes can be used within web applications
